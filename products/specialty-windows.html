Your Windows Aren’t Standard — Your Treatments Shouldn’t Be Either

WNC mountain homes are full of architectural character — soaring arches, angled rooflines, skylights, floor-to-ceiling glass, and windows in places no one planned to cover. We’ve been solving these challenges since 1992, and there isn’t a window shape we haven’t figured out.

Specialty windows are defined by anything with an arch or an angle — anything that’s not a standard rectangular opening. These require custom measurement, custom fabrication, and an installer who’s done it before. That’s us.

Arched Windows

Half-circle, quarter-circle, eyebrow, and full-arch windows. We custom-measure the exact curve and order treatments built to match — turning a challenging opening into a design feature. Options include shaped shutters, cellular shades, and specialty blinds.

Angled Windows

Triangular, trapezoidal, and raked-ceiling windows common in A-frame and mountain-contemporary homes. These require precise angle measurement and products engineered to operate on a slope. We handle all of it.

Skylights

Skylights need coverings too — for light control, heat management, and privacy in lofted bedrooms. Hunter Douglas offers skylight-specific cellular shades with motorization so you can control them from your phone or voice assistant. No ladders required.

Hard-to-Reach Windows

Two-story foyers, stairwells, clerestory windows above doors — WNC’s multi-level construction means windows in high places. Motorized treatments are the practical solution, and we install them every week. We also match window treatments to your existing décor, including custom wood stains and paint matching.
