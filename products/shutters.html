Factors to Consider When Choosing Shutters

Shutters are custom-fit to your window and offer many options: frame style, direct mounting, different louver sizes, custom colors and stains, standard tilt vs. hidden tilt, and privacy vs. view configurations.

Louver Sizes Matter

Traditional small louvers create an old-country aesthetic. Larger louvers (3.5" and 4.5") are more contemporary, give you a better view through, more light, and are easier to clean — perfect for small windows where you still want maximum view. Top and bottom louvers can be split to move independently for directional light control.

Frame Styles

Frame style is usually determined by the existing window and what's required to mount and complement the existing frame. Mounting requirements are assessed during your free consultation — we evaluate every window individually.

Room Environment

Consider décor and curb appeal, environmental exposure (sun and moisture), and the purpose they'll serve — privacy, light control, or insulation. Our team will help you choose the right material and configuration during your consultation.
