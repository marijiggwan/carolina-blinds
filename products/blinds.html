Types of Blinds We Carry

Every type, every style, every price point. See them all side by side in our Hendersonville or Weaverville showrooms.

Wood Blinds

Real hardwood with rich natural grain and warmth. Available in a range of paints, stains, and slat sizes (2", 2.5"). The premium choice for living rooms, bedrooms, and dining rooms where you want timeless elegance.

Basswood Custom Stains 2" & 2.5" Slats
Best for: Living rooms, bedrooms, dining rooms

Alternative Wood Blinds

The look of real wood with superior moisture and humidity resistance. Composite and PVC materials won't warp, crack, or fade — ideal for WNC's variable mountain climate and high-moisture rooms.

Moisture Resistant Won't Warp UV Resistant
Best for: Kitchens, bathrooms, humid areas

Aluminum (Metal) Blinds

Lightweight, durable, and available in more color options than any other blind type. An excellent choice for very wide windows where weight matters, and for modern aesthetics. Easy to clean and maintain.

Lightweight 50+ Colors ½" & 1" Slats
Best for: Wide windows, offices, modern spaces

Vertical & Track Blinds

The go-to solution for sliding glass doors, patio doors, and wide window walls. Available in fabric (free-hanging or PVC-backed), PVC, and aluminum. Smooth side-draw operation and excellent light control.

Fabric Options PVC & Aluminum Side-Draw
Best for: Sliding doors, patio doors, wide openings

Frequently Asked Questions About Blinds

Answers to the most common questions we hear from WNC homeowners.

Custom blind pricing depends on window size, material, and features. Wood blinds typically run $200–450 per window, alternative wood $150–350, and aluminum from $80–200. Vertical blinds for sliding doors range from $250–600. Motorization adds $150–300 per window. We carry options across every price point — from our Hunter Douglas Gallery collection to our Alta and Eclipse lines. The best way to get accurate pricing is to schedule a free in-home consultation where we measure your specific windows and provide a detailed estimate within 2–3 days.

Wood blinds are made from real hardwood (typically basswood) and offer a rich, natural grain. They're ideal for living rooms, bedrooms, and dining rooms. Alternative wood blinds use composite materials or PVC that mimic the look of real wood but resist moisture, warping, and fading — making them the better choice for kitchens, bathrooms, and the high-humidity areas common in WNC mountain homes. Both come in a wide range of paints, stains, and slat sizes. We always recommend seeing both side by side in our showroom or during an in-home consultation to feel the difference firsthand.

Absolutely — specialty and oversized windows are one of our areas of expertise. We regularly install blinds on arched windows, angled windows, skylights, bay windows, and extra-wide configurations common in WNC mountain homes. For very wide windows, we often recommend aluminum blinds (lighter weight = smoother operation) or split configurations. During your free consultation, we assess each window individually and recommend the best approach for both function and aesthetics.

From consultation to installation, most custom blind orders take 4–6 weeks. Some products and custom configurations may take 6–8 weeks depending on the manufacturer. We'll give you a specific timeline during your consultation. Installation itself is typically completed in one visit — a full house of blinds can usually be installed in a single day by our team. We handle all measuring, ordering, shipping, and installation so you don't have to coordinate anything.

Yes — we're specialists in motorized window treatments. Hunter Douglas PowerView® automation lets you control your blinds from your phone, tablet, remote, or voice assistant (Alexa, Google Home, Siri). You can set schedules so blinds open with sunrise and close at sunset automatically. We also offer Somfy motorization and can retrofit some existing blinds with motors. Motorized blinds are especially popular for hard-to-reach windows, large installations, and smart home setups. Visit our showroom to see live PowerView demonstrations.
